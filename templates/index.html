Quantum Computing

Quantum computing leverages the power of quantum bits (qubits), which can exist in superpositions of states, allowing them to solve problems exponentially faster than classical computers. Companies like IBM, Google, and IonQ are pushing the limits of hardware scalability and quantum error correction.

Quantum Entanglement

Quantum entanglement enables particles to remain correlated over vast distances. This foundational principle supports emerging technologies like quantum teleportation and quantum cryptography, forming the basis of future secure communication networks.

Photonic Quantum Networks

Photonic quantum computing uses light particles for computation, which travel faster and interact less with the environment, promising scalable and robust quantum systems. Researchers at Xanadu and academic labs are developing boson sampling systems that push the boundaries of photonic quantum hardware.

Applications of Quantum Research

Quantum technologies will revolutionize drug discovery, material science, finance, and logistics. Hybrid quantum-classical models are already helping tackle NP-hard problems and inspire new machine learning architectures.
